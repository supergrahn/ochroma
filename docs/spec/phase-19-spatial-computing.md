# Phase 19 — Spatial Computing & Mixed Reality

**Goal:** Make Ochroma the definitive engine for spatial computing — AR city planning on tabletops, VR city walkthroughs, and mixed reality collaborative editing.

## 19.1 Tabletop AR City Planning
- Project city onto a real table via passthrough AR
- Pinch to zoom, rotate with gestures
- Place buildings by tapping real-world surfaces
- See traffic and citizens moving on your table

## 19.2 VR City Walkthrough
- First-person walk through your city at street level
- Gaussian splats render naturally in stereo (no mesh aliasing)
- Hand tracking: grab and move buildings
- Teleport navigation between districts

## 19.3 Mixed Reality Collaborative
- Multiple headsets see the same city
- Each user's hand positions visible to others
- Spatial audio: hear other users' voices from their position
- Shared whiteboard for planning

## 19.4 Spatial UI
- UI panels float in 3D space
- Budget charts project above city hall
- Traffic heatmap as holographic overlay
- District boundaries as 3D walls

## Exit Criteria
- [ ] City visible on tabletop in AR passthrough
- [ ] VR walkthrough at street level with hand interaction
- [ ] Two VR users see each other's actions
- [ ] 3D UI panels render in world space
